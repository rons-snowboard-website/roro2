Taking Your Shred to the Next Level: Intermediate Snowboarding Tips

Conquered the bunny hills and ready to push your snowboarding skills further? Here's a guide for intermediate snowboarders to refine their technique, explore new terrain, and elevate their snowboarding experience:

Mastering Terrain:

  • Variety is Key: Venture beyond gentle slopes. Explore rolling terrain with steeper sections, moguls (small bumps), and wider trails to practice carving and speed control.

  • Carving Techniques: Focus on perfecting your carving turns. Practice initiating turns with proper body positioning, weight distribution, and edge control for smoother and more efficient riding.

  • Terrain Parks: If you're feeling adventurous, consider trying out terrain parks with jumps, rails, and other features. Start small and gradually progress to more challenging obstacles as your confidence grows. Safety first! Always assess your skill level and wear protective gear before attempting park features.

    • Developing Skills:

  • Ollie Pop: Learn the ollie pop, a fundamental jump that allows you to clear small obstacles or maneuver onto rails and boxes. Mastering this technique unlocks new possibilities in terrain parks and on the slopes.

  • Switch Riding: Practice riding "switch" (with your opposite foot forward). This improves balance, body control, and overall snowboarding proficiency. Start on flat ground and gradually transition to switch riding on gentle slopes.

  • Side Hits and Powder: If snow conditions permit, try tackling side hits (natural rollers on the side of the trail) and venturing into powder snow. These experiences refine your balance and edge control in different terrains.

    • Equipment Considerations:

  • Board Upgrade: As your skills progress, consider upgrading your snowboard to a stiffer flexing board that provides better stability and responsiveness at higher speeds and on steeper terrain.

  • Bindings and Boots: Ensure your bindings and boots are properly adjusted and offer the right level of support and flex for your riding style.

    • Safety and Awareness:

  • Helmet: Always wear a properly fitted helmet, no matter your skill level.

  • Protective Gear: Consider wrist guards and padded shorts for additional protection, especially when attempting new tricks or riding challenging terrain.

  • Mountain Awareness: Be aware of your surroundings, other skiers and snowboarders, and potential hazards on the slopes. Follow the code of conduct and safety guidelines set by the resort.

    • Additional Tips:

  • Video Analysis: Record yourself snowboarding and analyze your technique. This helps identify areas for improvement and track your progress.

  • Lessons or Clinics: Invest in a few lessons or clinics with a qualified instructor to receive personalized feedback and refine specific techniques.

  • Watch Pro Riders: Observe professional snowboarders and competitions to learn new techniques and get inspired.

  • Join the Community: Connect with other snowboarders, share experiences, and learn from each other.

    • Remember, progression takes time and dedication. Celebrate your achievements, embrace the learning process, and most importantly, have fun shredding the slopes! With consistent effort and the right approach, you'll be carving confident lines and exploring new heights on the mountain.
