Sella Ronda

The Sella Ronda, also known as the Sellaronda Ski Tour, is a world-famous circular ski route located in the Dolomites mountain range in northern Italy. It's a must-do for any skier or snowboarder visiting the region, offering stunning scenery and a unique skiing experience.

The Sella Ronda consists of four interconnected ski resorts: Val Gardena, Alta Badia, Arabba-Marmolada, and Val di Fassa. Skiers and snowboarders can complete the circuit in either direction, clockwise (known as the "Sellaronda Skimarathon") or counter-clockwise. The entire route is roughly 26 kilometers long, with a total elevation gain of about 4,500 meters.

Here are some of the highlights of the Sella Ronda:

  • Panoramic Views: The Sella Ronda is renowned for its breathtaking views of the Dolomites, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. As you ski around the circuit, you'll be surrounded by majestic peaks, towering rock faces, and idyllic valleys.

  • Variety of Slopes: The route offers a good mix of slopes to cater to skiers and snowboarders of all skill levels. There are plenty of gentle beginner slopes, challenging red runs for intermediate skiers, and even some black diamond slopes for experienced skiers.

  • Convenience: The Sella Ronda is very well-connected with a network of modern ski lifts and gondolas. You can easily ski from one resort to another without having to take off your skis.

  • Diverse Ski Resorts: Each of the four resorts that make up the Sella Ronda has its own unique character and charm. This allows you to experience the different vibes and offerings of each resort during your ski circuit.

    • Here are some additional details to consider about the Sella Ronda:

  • Direction: While you can ski the circuit in either direction, the clockwise route is generally considered to be easier, with more uphill sections on gondolas and less demanding slopes.

  • Time to Complete: The time it takes to complete the Sella Ronda depends on your skiing ability and how often you stop to rest and enjoy the views. A leisurely skier can complete the circuit in about 5-6 hours, while a more experienced skier can do it in 3-4 hours.

  • Skill Level: The Sella Ronda is generally considered to be suitable for intermediate skiers and above. However, there are sections that can be challenging even for experienced skiers, so it's important to be comfortable on red runs before attempting the full circuit.

  • Ski Pass: To ski the Sella Ronda, you'll need a Dolomiti Superski pass, which is valid for all the lifts in the region.

    • If you're looking for a challenging and scenic ski experience in the Dolomites, the Sella Ronda is definitely worth considering. Just be sure to choose the right direction for your skill level and allow enough time to complete the circuit comfortably.
