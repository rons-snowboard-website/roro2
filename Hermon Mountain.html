Hermon Mountain

Mount Hermon: Skiing on the Golan Heights

Mount Hermon, also known as Hermon Ski Resort, is a popular ski destination located on the Israeli-controlled side of the Golan Heights. While not as extensive as some of the other ski resorts mentioned, it offers a unique and scenic skiing experience in the Middle East.

Here's what you need to know about Mount Hermon:

  • Limited Season: Due to its lower elevation (compared to the Alps), the ski season at Mount Hermon is shorter, typically lasting from mid-December to mid-March, depending on snowfall.

  • Smaller Scale:The resort offers around 48 kilometers of ski slopes, catering primarily to beginner and intermediate skiers. There are some black diamond runs for experienced skiers, but the overall terrain is more limited compared to larger resorts.

  • Breathtaking Views: Despite the smaller scale, Mount Hermon boasts stunning views of the surrounding Golan Heights, the Israeli Hula Valley, and even glimpses of Mount Lebanon on clear days.

  • Family-Friendly: The resort is well-suited for families with children, offering gentle slopes, a dedicated learning area, and ski schools with qualified instructors.

  • Unique Location: Skiing in Israel offers a unique experience, combining winter sports with the opportunity to explore the historical and cultural attractions of the region.

    • Beyond the Slopes:

    While skiing is the main attraction, Mount Hermon offers some additional activities:

  • Snowshoeing: Explore the snowy landscapes on a guided snowshoe tour.

  • Sledding:Enjoy a fun-filled ride down the designated sledding area.

  • Snowball fights: Embrace the winter fun with snowball fights and snowman building.

    • Planning Your Trip to Mount Hermon:

  • Weather Conditions: Check the weather forecast and snow report before heading to the resort, as snow cover can be variable.

  • Limited Facilities: The resort has a limited number of restaurants and shops compared to larger ski destinations.

  • Transportation: Public transportation options are limited, so renting a car or booking a tour is recommended.

  • Accommodation: Several hotels and guesthouses are located near the resort, or you can choose to stay in nearby towns like Katzrin.

    • Overall, Mount Hermon offers a good option for a winter getaway in Israel, especially for families and beginner to intermediate skiers. The combination of skiing, stunning scenery, and the unique location makes it a memorable winter experience.

    Here are some additional points to consider:

  • Political Situation: Being located on the Golan Heights, it's advisable to stay informed about the current political situation before your trip.

  • Security: The Israeli side of the Golan Heights is generally considered safe for tourists. However, always follow safety precautions and be aware of your surroundings.
